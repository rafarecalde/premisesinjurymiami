Understanding Premises Liability in Miami

What you need to know about property injury claims in Florida.

Florida law requires property owners to maintain their premises in a reasonably safe condition for visitors. When they fail to do so, and someone is injured as a result, the property owner can be held financially responsible through a premises liability claim.

Who Can Be Held Liable?

Premises injury cases in Miami often involve multiple responsible parties. Unlike a simple car accident with one at-fault driver, property injury claims can target:

  • Property owners — the individual or entity that owns the land and structures
  • Property management companies — third-party firms hired to maintain and operate the property
  • Commercial tenants — businesses leasing space who control their portion of the premises
  • Homeowners associations — condo and community associations responsible for common areas
  • Maintenance contractors — companies hired for specific upkeep like landscaping, cleaning, or repairs
  • Construction companies — developers and contractors whose work creates hazards for the public

Why Property Injury Claims Often Carry Significant Value

Commercial and residential real estate properties in Miami are high-value assets. The entities that own and manage these properties carry insurance policies that reflect that value. A single apartment complex, hotel, or shopping center may have general liability coverage of one million dollars or more, plus umbrella policies that extend well beyond that.

When multiple responsible parties are involved, multiple insurance policies may be available to compensate you. This is a key difference between premises injury cases and many other personal injury claims.

Act Quickly: Florida's statute of limitations gives you two years to file a premises liability lawsuit. But the real deadline is much shorter. Surveillance footage is overwritten in days or weeks. Maintenance records are discarded. Witnesses move away. The sooner you contact an attorney, the more evidence we can preserve to strengthen your case.

What Compensation Can You Recover?

A successful premises injury claim in Miami can recover compensation for:

  • Current and future medical expenses, including surgery, rehabilitation, and ongoing care
  • Lost wages and loss of future earning capacity
  • Pain and suffering
  • Emotional distress and psychological trauma
  • Loss of enjoyment of life
  • Scarring and disfigurement
  • Wrongful death damages for surviving family members

Every case is different, and the value of your claim depends on the severity of your injuries, the strength of the evidence, and the insurance coverage available. The best way to understand what your case may be worth is to speak with an attorney who handles premises injury cases in Miami.
